A rule, or your own hand

There are four building tools, and only one thing separates them: who decides the shape. With Block, Row houses and Detached, the shape comes out of a rule you set with sliders. With Freeform, you draw exactly what you want.

All four end up as an urban rule proposal — they differ only in typology (Block, Row, Parcel-based). This is not some abstract regulation: the proposal carries the actual building footprints you generated.
Block and Row houses need contiguous parcels. If they aren't, the tools are disabled and tell you why: “Disabled because the parcels in the proposal are not contiguous”. Row houses also need at least two parcels — on a single one it is just an ordinary building.

Block — “Urban Rule”

Fills a whole block with perimeter development — buildings along the edge, courtyard in the middle. The tool opens the Urban Rule window with a map, a 3D preview and a Parameters panel.

Two ways to work

The Based on existing buildings toggle changes the whole parameter panel:

  • Off (the default) — the block is drawn from scratch, out of the sliders.
  • On — it keeps the existing buildings and only changes their height. You choose between Proposed height (floors) (every building goes to exactly that height) and Additional floors (each one gets that many floors added on top of what it has). Floor height (m) runs 2.5–5 m.

The sliders (drawing from scratch)

Parameter Range What it does
Setback 0–50 m Distance from the parcel boundary.
Building Width 1–100 m Depth of the perimeter wing. Wider = smaller courtyard.
Building Height 3–80 m Height of the perimeter development.
Chamfer 0–10 m Cuts the corners.
Simplify 0–20 m Smooths out a ragged outline.
Number and width of gaps 0–10 · 1–20 m Openings through the perimeter wing (passages, courtyard entrances).
Number and length of wings 0–10 · 10–200 m Wings reaching into the courtyard.
You don't have to guess gaps and wings with the sliders. As soon as there is at least one, handles appear on the map — “Drag to move gap” and “Drag to move wing” — and you slide them along the perimeter until they sit exactly where they should.

If the rule won't give you what you want, click Edit shape manually: every vertex of the outline becomes a handle you can drag (“Drag to reshape”). The way back is Back to sliders — but that discards your manual edits. You can also bring in an existing footprint with Upload GeoJSON.

Confirm with Done. The button stays inactive until a building has been generated.

The courtyard can vanish: if the building is too wide, the inner edge falls apart and you get a solid block. The message says as much — the fix is to reduce the Building Width. On a very complicated parcel the tool may give up entirely and suggest you split it with roads first.

Row houses

A terrace of attached houses across several parcels. The Row Houses window has four sliders: Length (4–200 m), Width (2–100 m), Height (3–80 m) and Chamfer (0–10 m, cuts the corners at 45°).

Length runs parallel to the longest side of the parcel, width perpendicular to it. You see Circumference, Area and Volume update live.

The row is directly manipulable on the map. Drag the body to move it, the orange corner handle to rotate it, and the black handles at the midpoints of the sides to change its width and length.
The row has to stay inside the selected parcels. Ask for a value that doesn't fit and the tool first tries to shift and straighten the row — and if that isn't enough, the slider snaps back to the last valid value. That's not a bug, that's the edge of the site.

Detached houses

One standalone house on each selected parcel. Only two sliders:

  • Max Floors (1–20) — each house gets a random number of floors between 1 and that value.
  • Min Distance from Borders (0.5–20 m) — the setback from the boundaries.

The panel shows Buildings, Total Footprint, Total Volume and Avg Floors.

The randomness is deliberate — it makes the neighbourhood look organic instead of like a row of identical boxes. Don't like the arrangement? Regenerate rolls the dice again: different heights, different footprint sizes and positions within the setbacks.

Freeform

For when no rule suits you. The Buildings window has no urban logic in it — just rectangles you place yourself. And it isn't limited to one building: at the top there's a list of buildings with buttons to add, rename and delete, so you can assemble several.

For the selected building the sliders are Width and Length (1–100 m), Height (3–250 m), Chamfer (0–10 m) and Rotation (0–355°). You can also simply drag the building around the map, and rotate it with the handles on its sides.

The building has to stay inside the block. As with row houses, a value that would push it out is rejected and the slider snaps back.
Start with a rule, finish by hand. Block gives you a convincing perimeter block in seconds; if it gets stuck on one detail, switch to Edit shape manually instead of fighting the sliders. Save Freeform for the cases where you are genuinely designing, not applying a rule.
