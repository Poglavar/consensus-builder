Pool it, then divide it again

Land readjustment merges the selected parcels into one surface and then re-divides it into a tidier layout. This is not merge-then-split by hand: the ownership shares are preserved, and the app shows you who put in how much and how much they get back.

  1. Select contiguous parcels

    Turn on Select multiple parcels and mark the block you want to rearrange. The parcels must be contiguous — otherwise the submission fails.

  2. Start the tool

    In the Proposals tab, in the “Build” palette, click Land readjustment. The Land readjustment window opens and immediately proposes a layout.

  3. Pick the type

    Under Land readjustment type you choose between two options:

    Sweep line algorithm Automatically draws parallel division lines and lays out the new plots. The default, and the fastest.
    Manual You draw each new plot yourself and assign it an owner.

  4. Refine the layout

    Sweep line: a handle appears on the map — “Drag to rotate the parcel lines”. Rotating it changes the direction of the division and you see a different layout immediately. When the land is in the hands of a single owner, you also get Number of parcels and Distribution — Equal, Random or Manual.

    Manual: this enables the Draw plot tool (click the vertices, then Finish plot) and Split with line (drag a cut through an existing plot). You can also import an existing plan with Upload GeoJSON. Then click each new plot and assign it an owner.

    The app keeps track of whether all the land is covered. Until it is, it reports how much is not yet assigned and how many plots have no owner. The goal is “All pooled land assigned (100%).”

  5. Check the owner balance

    This is the heart of a readjustment. For each owner, the table shows:

    Contributed How much land they put into the pool.
    Assigned How much they get in the new layout.
    Balance The difference. This is the number people will negotiate over.
    Cash offer Money to compensate the ones who come out short.

    Owner Legend explains the colours on the map, and the Before / After toggle compares the old layout with the new one.

    A readjustment that leaves nobody short of what they put in practically votes itself through. If someone's Balance is negative, either fix their share or offer them cash — otherwise you have no reason to expect them to agree.

  6. Confirm with “Done”

    Click Done to save the layout into the proposal. This step is mandatory — without it the proposal cannot be created.

  7. Write the terms and create it

    Create Proposal opens: name, description and offer. Click Create Proposal and you're done.

Land readjustment changes the shape of the parcels but keeps the ownership shares — which is why it is the right tool for badly divided blocks: nobody loses land, they just get it back in a more usable form.
