The space first, the furniture second

Public space appears in one click — no editor, no parameters. A park, a square or a lake covers the selected parcels immediately. Only afterwards, if you want, do you open the editor and fill it with trees, benches, paths and fountains.

  1. Select the parcels and click the tool

    Select the parcels (or a whole block), open the Proposals tab and, in the “Build” palette, click Park, Square or Lake. The space is applied at once, over the merged outline of the selected parcels.

    A square doesn't start out empty. It comes with a fountain in the middle, two to four tables along the edges, and a statue. A park starts as a plain green surface — everything on it is your doing. A lake requires the parcels to be contiguous.

  2. Open the editor

    Furniture gets added after the proposal exists. Open the park or square proposal and click Edit geometry. The Park editor panel (or Square editor) appears along the bottom of the map, and the boundary of the space is drawn as a dashed line.

    The editor doesn't live in a window — it works directly on the map, so you can zoom and pan while you place things.

  3. Place the furniture

    The instruction in the panel says it all: “Choose an item, then click inside the boundary. Drag point items to move them.”

    Park How you place it
    🌳 Tree One click. Drag it to move it.
    ▰ Bench One click.
    🌸 Flowerbed A circle — the whole of it must fit inside the boundary.
    💧 Pond A circle, same rule.
    〰 Footpath Click the points, then Finish path (or Enter).
    Square How you place it
    ⛲ Fountain One click.
    🌳 Tree One click.
    ▰ Bench One click.
    🍽️ Table One click — a terrace table, for cafés and restaurants.
    🗿 Statue One click.

    To delete something, pick Remove and click the item.

    Everything has to be inside the dashed boundary. Click outside it and you get “Place items inside the structure boundary.”
    Zoom in before you place a pond or a flowerbed. Their size depends on the current zoom level: close up you get small, precise items, far out you get big ones.

  4. Save the design

    Click Save design. The confirmation reads “Structure design saved.” Cancel throws away everything you placed in that session.

  5. Check it in 3D

    Everything you placed also exists in the 3D view — the trees, the benches, the fountains and the statues stand exactly where you clicked them. A park with no trees looks tidy in plan and empty in 3D; that's where the difference shows.

Public space is the cheapest way to make a proposal attractive to the neighbours. A park next to a proposed block of buildings is often the difference between a proposal that passes and one that doesn't.
