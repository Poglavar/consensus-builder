02
Primitive

Every action is a proposal.

Sell, split, merge, lease, rezone, build, demolish, donate, swap. All the same primitive — a geometry, a description, a lens, signatures.

SALE

Owner → Buyer. €240k. Contingent on bank attestation.

SPLIT

One parcel becomes two. Inherits prior attestations.

BUILD

3-storey residential, 24 units, energy class A.

MERGE

Three adjacent parcels become one. All owners co-sign.

PARK

Convert to public green space. Funded by 142 neighbors.

Because everything is a proposal, everything is composable. A development deal is a graph of proposals signed by the right parties under the right lens.

04
Trust

The Lens.
Your worldview, configurable.

A lens is a list of addresses you trust to attest. It's how you turn a noisy substrate of everybody saying everything into a coherent picture of who owns what.

Different lenses produce different worlds:

  • Cadastre lens — the official registry's view. The legal ground truth.
  • Neighborhood lens — what the locals say. Catches squatting, informal use.
  • Bank lens — what financiers will lend against.
  • Custom lens — your AI agent, your three favorite notaries, your DAO.

One click switches your entire view of the city.

Lenses overlap. Where they agree is uncontested. Where they diverge is where the work is.

07
Use case — Public

Public goods, finally with a tooling layer.

A neighborhood wants a park. Today this takes 4 years and dies in committee. With consensus-builder:

  1. An AI agent drafts a multi-parcel proposal: which land, what design, who pays, what's the maintenance plan.
  2. The proposal is published under a civic lens — municipality, urbanist, ward representative, the school next door.
  3. Residents read, comment, counter-propose. Each amendment is a child proposal — visible, comparable.
  4. When endorsements reach the threshold encoded in the lens, the proposal becomes binding. Funds release.

Same rails for: pedestrianisation, school zoning, social housing, public transit corridors, urban renewal.

A pocket park, assembled from 7 parcels under a civic lens.
08
Use case — Private

Private development at machine speed.

A developer wants to assemble a 12-parcel site for a mixed-use project. Today this takes 18 months of holdouts and lawyers. With consensus-builder:

  1. AI scout finds candidate sites matching FAR, zoning, transit access, comparable sales.
  2. Per-parcel offer proposals are drafted, each priced from the bank-lens valuation.
  3. Owner agents respond — accept, counter, refer to family. The graph fills in over days, not quarters.
  4. A bundled proposal — "all 12 or none" — closes atomically when every owner has signed.

No holdouts can stall the deal silently. Counterparty agents are on the clock; proposals expire.

Assembly grid, day 4 of 14.

11
Timing

Why now.

01

Signatures are free

Solana + L2 attestation costs are sub-cent. A property's lifetime can hold tens of thousands of attestations without economic friction.

02

Agents can read deeds

Frontier models reliably parse legal text, planning documents, and cadastre records. The expensive professionals just got commoditised.

03

Soulbound is normalised

Non-transferable NFTs are a familiar pattern. The "but tokens must be transferable" objection has faded.

04

Regulators are listening

MiCA, Article 6 RWA frameworks, and several national land registry pilots are explicitly looking for attestation-shaped tooling.
